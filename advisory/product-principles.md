# Product Principles

## 1. Advisory Only

Quilvar is a decision-support system, not an auto-execution system.

No automated trading, no autonomous position changes, and no external claims that imply the system operates as a trading bot.

## 2. Credibility Before Sophistication

Reliable ingestion, storage, validation, and auditability matter more than adding more models, feeds, or features.

If the underlying history is wrong, model sophistication only produces more confident errors.

## 3. Measure What Matters

The product should optimize for:
- reliability
- relevance
- analyst usefulness
- validated outcomes

Not for:
- impressive demos without ground truth
- high alert volume
- speculative claims about predictive power

## 4. Separate Intent From Reality

Always distinguish:
- intended architecture
- implemented behavior
- validated performance

The system should never be marketed or operated based on aspiration alone.

## 5. Human Workflow Wins First

The first product win is helping humans see important events faster and in a more structured way.

Human workflow improvement comes before algorithmic authority.

## 6. Historical Record Is a Strategic Asset

The long-term defensibility is not the prompt. It is the validated signal history.

Protect data quality, schema consistency, and outcome integrity accordingly.

## 7. Launch in Layers

Recommended progression:
- internal shadow mode
- internal pilot with measured usage
- limited design-partner access
- broader release only after reliability and validation thresholds are met

## 8. Earn Stronger Claims

Do not claim predictive edge, alpha, or decision-grade reliability until the system has:
- real market-price integration
- trustworthy validation semantics
- stable operations
- enough historical evidence to support the claim
